# The Counterfeit of the Sublime: On the Pathological Structure and Sociological Metaphor of "Performative Idealism"    

— *Taking the Yu Xiaoqing Archetype as a Point of Departure*

**Publication Date**: December 12, 2025
**Author**: Ailven LIU 刘翔
**All Rights Reserved**

In the television drama series *Soldiers and Their Commander* (in Chinese named as 我的团长我的团), the character of Yu Xiaoqing provides a socio-psychological specimen of penetrating insight. He is not a hypocrite in the traditional sense, but rather a "Performative Idealist" who has achieved a high degree of psychological self-consistency. The core characteristic of this personality type lies in **alienating idealism into an aesthetic ornament and an extension of power; exhibiting extreme romance and sublimity in zones free of conflict of interest, yet instantaneously switching to extreme instrumental rationality when core interests are triggered, while sanctifying this betrayal through a rigorous logical system.**

This personality is not exclusive to the elite class; rather, it resembles a collective symptom widely diffused throughout modern society, particularly among groups gripped by the anxiety of class mobility. This article aims to dissect the deep-seated psychological mechanisms and sociological essence of this "sophisticated egoism wrapped in an idealistic shell" through the mirror of Yu Xiaoqing.

## I. The Dramaturgical Perspective: The Accumulation and Display of Moral Capital

From the perspective of Erving Goffman’s "Dramaturgical Theory," the life of a Yu Xiaoqing-style figure is essentially a meticulously choreographed "front stage" performance. For this category of individuals, idealism, patriotism, and humanistic concern are not internalized faiths, but rather critical stage properties required to construct their "persona."

Yu Xiaoqing’s study, his superior weaponry, and his reverence for General Yue Fei constitute the scenography of his performance. In modern society, this corresponds to performative philanthropy on social media, vision statements in the corporate world, or the middle class’s paranoid imitation of "high culture" lifestyles. Pierre Bourdieu pointed out that cultural and moral capital can be converted into power. These individuals are well-versed in this alchemy; by high-profile proselytizing of idealism, they accumulate "moral capital," thereby establishing superiority and legitimacy within the group.

This performance possesses a potent deceptive quality because, in the absence of conflicting interests, they do indeed invest genuine emotion. They are moved by the sense of sublimity they cultivate. However, this emotion is authentic yet **narcissistic**. They do not love the masses; they love the "self who loves the masses." This idealism, based on aesthetics rather than ethics, is destined to be fragile and superficial. It provides the halo but refuses to bear the cost.

## II. Instrumental Rationality and the "Reification" of Others: The Cold Logic Behind the Veil of Warmth

When this personality type faces a choice involving interests, their psychological mechanism undergoes a violent rupture and reconstruction. This is the ultimate embodiment of "Instrumental Reason" as critiqued by the Frankfurt School.

When core interests (such as position, wealth, or class security) remain untouched, their attitude towards others is often affectionate, brimming with brotherhood or team spirit. However, once the existence of another becomes an obstacle, or if sacrificing another can yield significant personal gain, the "flesh-and-blood human" is instantly **"reified" (Verdinglichung)** in their cognition.

In the drama, this means living soldiers become flags on a sandbox; in modern professional or social relationships, it means erstwhile allies instantly transform into disposable pawns. The terror of this transformation lies in its **seamless abruptness**. One moment, they are discussing poetry and distant horizons over wine; the next, they are coolly calculating the yield rate of betrayal.

This stark contrast reveals the desolation at the core of their personality: **Others are never ends, but merely means.** They lack what Martin Buber termed the "I-Thou" relationship, engaging only in an "I-It" relationship of utility. All idealistic rhetoric is merely sophisticated packaging designed to make this process of utilization appear less voracious.

## III. Sartrean "Bad Faith": The Legitimization Narrative of Betrayal

Why can such individuals maintain a stance of righteousness, or even tragedy, while practicing self-interest (such as sacrificing subordinates or acting perfidiously)? This involves the concept of **"Bad Faith" (Mauvaise foi)** proposed by Jean-Paul Sartre.

Yu Xiaoqing-style figures cannot endure the self-perception of being base; thus, they must construct a grand narrative to conceal their private desires. When they choose betrayal for personal gain, they do not say, "I want a promotion" or "I am afraid of losing"; they say, "This is for the greater good," "This is for long-term strategy," or "This is the price of growth."

They excel at escaping moral anxiety through **conceptual substitution**:
- Substituting "personal greed" with "collective interest";
- Substituting "cold-blooded calculation" with "mature rationality";
- Substituting "exploitation of the weak" with "the law of natural selection."

Through this psychological defense mechanism, they successfully reshape themselves from perpetrators into "burden-bearing martyrs." They may even weep for the "difficult decisions" they are forced to make, appearing more pained than their victims. This performative suffering is the most sophisticated camouflage of the refined egoist—it not only dissolves the legitimacy of the victim's grievance but further reinforces the moral invincibility of the perpetrator.

## IV. Social Pathology Under the Anxiety of Class Mobility

Expanding our horizon to the broader modern society, the proliferation of the Yu Xiaoqing personality is, in reality, a product of the **anxiety surrounding class mobility**. In a highly competitive and atomized social structure, many who pursue "upward mobility" or a "refined life" are forced, or actively choose, to mutate into this personality type.

For those yearning to climb the ladder, pure idealism is an expensive luxury, while being an unvarnished villain invites social exclusion. Therefore, the "Machiavellian in saint’s clothing" becomes the optimal evolutionary strategy.

1. **Elite Mimicry:** They imitate the tone, aesthetics, and values of the upper class (just as Yu imitates Yue Fei), attempting to obtain an entry ticket to the class via the synchronization of cultural symbols.
2. **Defensive Indifference:** Deep down, they lack security and view the world as a zero-sum game. Consequently, all "sentiments" have a stop-loss limit; once personal safety (interests) is touched, the survival instinct immediately commandeers the brain, and empathy for others resets to zero.

The pervasive existence of this personality in modern society leads to a **systemic collapse of trust**. People appear to be enthusiastically exchanging visions, while in reality, everyone is clutching a dagger in the shadows, ready to cut the rope at any moment.

## Conclusion: The Triumph of Nihilism

Yu Xiaoqing-style figures, and the countless "micro-Yu Xiaoqings" in modern society, appear to be the victors of rationality. They enjoy the reputational halo brought by idealism while harvesting the tangible fruits of realism.

However, from the perspective of existentialist philosophy, this is a state of utter nihilism. They live within layers of lies and masks, ultimately losing the ability to perceive the real world. They deceive not only the audience but eventually themselves. When one becomes accustomed to viewing everyone around them (including comrades, family, and subordinates) as "fuel" for their objectives, they effectively imprison themselves in a labyrinth composed of mirrors—where there is no true "Other," merely infinite projections of a self consumed by desire and fear.

This "sophisticated egoism" may be tactically invincible, but strategically, it is a devastating blow to the foundation of humanity. It manufactures a world that is magnificent yet frigid, where all sublimity is a synonym for calculation, and behind every tear-filled eye lies a cold, calculating abacus.
